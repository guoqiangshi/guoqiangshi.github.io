Biography

I am currently an Assistant Professor with the Department of Land Surveying and Geo-Informatics (LSGI) , The Hong Kong Polytechnic University.

My research interests include interferometric synthetic aperture radar (InSAR) remote sensing for urban geohazards monitoring and modelling, urban geophysics (groundwater, coastal reclamation; landslides, built environment), and infrastructure health diagnosis. I am currently working on slope instability assessment in the forested and mountainous regions of Hong Kong, and the Greater Bay Area of China.

I received my PhD degree in Earth System and GeoInformation Science from the Chinese University of Hong Kong in 2019. During 2018 and 2019, I was a visiting PhD at the Department of Earth and Planetary Science, University of California - Berkeley. I was a postdoctoral fellow with the Institute of Space and Earth Information Science (ISEIS), CUHK from 2019 to 2022, and Research Assistant Professor with LSGI, PolyU, from 2022 to 2024.
